Signature Dishes from Different States

Kerala - Sadya (Traditional Feast)

"Sadya" is a traditional and grand feast in the South Indian state of Kerala, often served on special occasions, festivals, and weddings.

1.Set Up Banana Leaf: Lay a fresh banana leaf as a serving plate, traditionally used in Sadya.
2.Main Course: Place hot, boiled white rice in the center of the banana leaf.
3.Side Dishes: Surround the rice with an assortment of traditional Kerala side dishes, including sambar, avial, thorans, pickles, and more.
4.Dessert and Buttermilk: Conclude the meal with a sweet dessert like payasam and serve spiced buttermilk to aid digestion.

Since Sadya consists of numerous dishes, it's recommended to follow specific recipes for each item to create an authentic Kerala Sadya.

Punjab - Sarson Saag and Makki Roti

Makki Roti with Sarson Saag is a classic and iconic Punjabi dish, renowned for its hearty and rustic flavors.

1.Blanch mustard greens, spinach, and bathua until tender.
2.Blend them into a fine paste.
3.Sauté ginger, garlic, green chilies, and spices in ghee or oil.
4.Add the green paste and simmer until flavors meld.

Tamil Nadu - Chettinad Chicken Curry

Chettinad Chicken is a renowned South Indian dish known for its bold and aromatic flavors.

1.Marinate Chicken : Marinate bone-in chicken in yogurt and Chettinad spice mix.
2.Prepare Curry Base:
Sauté onions, ginger, garlic in oil until golden.
Add tomato puree and cook until soft.
3.Cook Chicken:
Add marinated chicken, cook until tender.
Add coconut and tamarind for flavor.
4.Temper for Aroma:
Temper mustard seeds and curry leaves separately.
Add to the curry for enhanced aroma.

Rajasthan - Dal Baati Churma

Dal Baati Churma is a traditional and iconic Rajasthani dish that consists of three main components: dal, baati, and churma

1.Mix wheat flour and ghee, knead into a firm dough with water.
2.Shape into small round baatis, then flatten slightly.
3.Bake in an oven or over an open flame until golden brown and crispy.
4.Cook lentils until soft, then temper with spices like mustard seeds, cumin, and dried red chilies.

Bihar - Litti Chokha

Litti Chokha is a traditional and rustic dish that originates from the Indian state of Bihar

1.Prepare a firm dough by mixing whole wheat flour and water.
2.Divide the dough into small portions and shape them into balls.
3.Hollow out each ball and stuff it with a mixture of roasted gram flour (sattu), spices, and herbs.
4.Roast the stuffed littis over an open flame or in a tandoor until golden brown and crisp.

Uttarakhand - Aloo Ke Gutke

Aloo Ke Gutke is a popular and flavorful North Indian dish, particularly enjoyed in the state of Uttarakhand

1.Fry Potatoes : Dice and fry potatoes until crispy.
2.Season : Add spices like red chili, turmeric, and coriander
3.Add Aromatic Herbs : Incorporate dried fenugreek leaves and fresh curry leaves.
4.Serve Hot : Enjoy as a tasty side dish or snack.

Mizoram - Bai

Bai is a traditional dish from the northeastern Indian state of Manipur. It's a unique and flavorful preparation that features a combination of leafy greens, vegetables, and bamboo shoots cooked with a special fermented ingredient known as "hawaijar."

1.Boil Ingredients : Boil leafy greens, vegetables, and bamboo shoots until tender.
2.Sauté with Spices : Sauté boiled veggies with spices, ginger, and garlic until cooked.
3.Add Hawaijar : Mix in hawaijar (fermented soybean product) for flavor.
4.Simmer : Simmer until flavors meld, and the dish is ready.

Madhya Pradesh - Poha

Poha, also known as flattened rice or beaten rice, is a popular and traditional breakfast dish in India

1.Rinse flattened rice (poha) in a sieve.
2.Heat oil in a pan, add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, and curry leaves.
3.Sauté onions until translucent.
4.Add turmeric powder, chopped green chilies, and salt.
5.Mix in rinsed poha and cook for a few minutes.
6.Garnish with cilantro and serve with lemon wedges.

Karnataka - Bisi Bele Bhat

Bisi Bele Bhat is a traditional South Indian rice dish that boasts a harmonious blend of flavors and textures

1.Cook rice and toor dal (split pigeon peas) separately.
2.Sauté mixed vegetables in oil.
3.Add bisi bele bhat powder and tamarind paste.
4.Combine cooked dal, cooked rice, and the vegetable mixture.
5.Tempering: Heat ghee, add spices, and pour over the dish.
6.Garnish with coriander leaves and fried nuts.

Andhara Pradesh - Andhra Chicken Curry

Andhra Chicken Curry is a spicy and flavorful dish hailing from the southern Indian state of Andhra Pradesh

1.Marinate chicken with spices and yogurt.
2.Sauté onions, add ginger-garlic paste and chilies.
3.Add marinated chicken and cook until browned.
4.Mix in tamarind paste and simmer until chicken is tender.
5.Garnish with curry leaves and serve hot.

Gujarat - Dhokla

Dhokla is a popular and savory steamed cake from the Indian state of Gujarat.

1.Mix chickpea flour, yogurt, and spices to make batter.
2.Steam batter until set (15-20 min).
3.Cut into pieces, temper with mustard seeds and curry leaves.
4.Garnish with coriander; serve with chutney.

Tripura - Mui Borok

Mui Borok is a traditional and pungent condiment from the Indian state of Manipur, known for its strong and distinctive flavor.

1.Sun-dry and finely grind fish.
2.Mix with salt and optional spices.
3.Ferment until desired flavor (several weeks).
4.Store in airtight container for use as a condiment.

Odisha - Bodi Chura

Bodi Chura, also known as "Badi Chura," is a traditional dish from the Indian state of Bihar. It is a flavorful and crunchy preparation made using sun-dried lentil dumplings called "bodi."

1.Crush sun-dried bodi (lentil dumplings).
2.Mix with mustard oil, garlic, green chilies, and spices.
3.Optional: Add onions and tomatoes.
4.Serve as a side dish with rice or roti.

Delhi - Chole Bhature

Chole Bhature is a popular North Indian dish known for its combination of spicy chickpea curry (chole) and deep-fried bread (bhature).

1.Cook chickpeas in spices and tomato gravy.
2.Make dough for bhature (fried bread).
3.Roll and deep-fry bhature.
4.Serve hot with chickpea curry (chole).

Haryana - Bajra Khichdi

Bajra Khichdi is a wholesome and nutritious dish from India, made primarily from bajra (pearl millet) and moong dal (split green gram).

1.Cook bajra and moong dal with water.
2.Sauté spices, vegetables, and add to the cooked grains.
3.Simmer until well blended.
4.Serve hot with yogurt or ghee.

Goa - Fish Curry Rice

Fish Curry Rice is a classic coastal Indian dish renowned for its delicious and tangy flavors.

1.Marinate fish with spices.
2.Simmer fish in tomato-tamarind curry.
3.Serve hot with steamed rice.

Arunachal Pradesh - Thukpa

Thukpa is a hearty and flavorful Tibetan noodle soup that has also become popular in parts of India

1.Boil noodles separately.
2.Prepare a savory broth with spices, vegetables, and meat/tofu.
3.Combine noodles and broth.
4.Garnish with cilantro and lime; serve hot.

Manipur - Eromba

Eromba is a traditional Manipuri dish known for its bold and spicy flavors.

1.Boil vegetables with fermented fish.
2.Crush chilies and herbs.
3.Mix all ingredients; serve with rice.
