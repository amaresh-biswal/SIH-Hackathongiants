Package 1: Maharastra

Maharashtra is a state in western India that offers a rich cultural and natural diversity. Here is a possible itinerary for a 5 days trip to Maharashtra:

Day 1: Arrive in Mumbai, the capital city and the financial hub of India. Explore the iconic landmarks, such as the Gateway of India, the Chhatrapati Shivaji Terminus, and the Mani Bhavan, where Mahatma Gandhi stayed. Enjoy the street food and nightlife of Mumbai.

Day 2: Take a day trip to Lonavala, a scenic hill station about 90 km from Mumbai. Visit the Karla Caves, a complex of ancient Buddhist rock-cut temples. Enjoy the panoramic views of the Western Ghats and the waterfalls.

Day 3: Drive to Nashik, a holy city on the banks of the Godavari river. Visit the Trimbakeshwar Temple, one of the twelve Jyotirlingas of Lord Shiva. Explore the vineyards and wineries of Nashik, and taste some of the finest wines in India.

Day 4: Drive to Aurangabad, a historical city that is the gateway to the Ajanta and Ellora caves. Visit the Ajanta Caves, a UNESCO World Heritage Site that showcases exquisite paintings and sculptures of Buddhist art. Visit the Ellora Caves, another UNESCO World Heritage Site that features rock-cut temples of Hinduism, Buddhism, and Jainism.

Day 5: Return to Mumbai and depart for your onward journey.

Package 2: Tamil Nadu

Tamil Nadu is a state in south India with rich culture and nature. Here is a possible itinerary for a 3 days trip:

Day 1: Arrive in Chennai, the capital and financial hub of India. Explore the landmarks, such as the Gateway of India, the Chhatrapati Shivaji Terminus, and the Mani Bhavan. Enjoy the food and nightlife of Chennai.

Day 2: Take a day trip to Mahabalipuram, a coastal town with UNESCO sites. Visit the Shore Temple and the Five Rathas, rock-cut temples with carvings. Relax at the Blue Flag Beach, a clean and safe beach.

Day 3: Drive to Pondicherry, a former French colony with colonial charm. Visit the Manakula Vinayagar Temple, a Hindu temple of Lord Ganesha. Explore the Botanical Gardens and the Pondicherry Museum.

Package 3: Delhi

Delhi is a city of contrasts, where ancient and modern coexist. Here is a possible itinerary for a 3 days trip:

Day 1: Arrive in Delhi and check in to your hotel. Visit the Red Fort, a UNESCO site and the Mughal residence. Explore the Chandni Chowk, a bustling market with food, clothes, and souvenirs.

Day 2: Take a day trip to Agra, about 200 km from Delhi. Visit the Taj Mahal, another UNESCO site and a symbol of love and beauty. Also visit the Agra Fort, a majestic fort with Mughal architecture and history.

Day 3: Visit the Qutub Minar, the tallest brick minaret in the world and a masterpiece of Islamic art. Visit the Lotus Temple, a stunning white marble structure that welcomes people of all faiths. Visit the India Gate, a war memorial and a landmark of Delhi.

Package 4: Himachal Pradesh

Himachal Pradesh is a state in northern India that offers scenic beauty, cultural diversity, and adventure activities. Here is a possible itinerary for a 7 days trip to Himachal Pradesh:

Day 1: Arrive in Chandigarh and drive to Shimla, the capital of Himachal Pradesh and a popular hill station. Check in to your hotel and explore the Mall Road, the Ridge, and the Jakhu Temple.

Day 2: Take a day trip to Kufri, a nearby town that offers snow activities, wildlife park, and panoramic views. Return to Shimla and visit the Viceregal Lodge, the former residence of the British Viceroy.

Day 3: Drive to Manali, another famous hill station that is known for its natural beauty and adventure sports. En route, visit the Pandoh Dam, the Beas-Sutlej Link Project, and the Kullu Valley. Check in to your hotel and relax.

Day 4: Visit the Hadimba Devi Temple, a unique wooden temple dedicated to a mythological character. Also visit the Manu Temple, the Vashisht Temple, and the Tibetan Monastery. Enjoy shopping at the Mall Road and the Old Manali market.

Day 5: Take a day trip to Solang Valley, a picturesque spot that offers paragliding, skiing, zorbing, and ropeway rides. You can also visit the Rohtang Pass, a high mountain pass that connects Manali to Lahaul and Spiti valleys (subject to weather and permit conditions).

Day 6: Drive to Dharamshala, a serene town that is home to the Dalai Lama and the Tibetan government-in-exile. En route, visit the Baijnath Temple, a 13th-century temple dedicated to Lord Shiva. Check in to your hotel and enjoy the evening at leisure.

Day 7: Visit the McLeod Ganj, a suburb of Dharamshala that is also known as Little Lhasa. Visit the Namgyal Monastery, the Tsuglagkhang Complex, the Bhagsunag Temple, and the Dal Lake. Drive back to Chandigarh and depart for your onward journey.

Package 5: Assam

Assam is a state in Northeast India that offers breathtaking natural beauty and cultural diversity. Here is a possible itinerary for a 3 days trip to Assam:

Day 1: Arrive in Guwahati, the gateway to Northeast India. Visit the Kamakhya Temple, a historic and religious site. Explore the Srimanta Sankardev Kalakshetra, a cultural complex of Assam.

Day 2: Take a day trip to Kaziranga National Park, a UNESCO site and the home of the rhino. Enjoy a safari and spot wildlife. Also visit the Kaziranga National Orchid and Biodiversity Park, a garden of orchids and plants.

Day 3: Drive to Pobitora Wildlife Sanctuary, a reserve with the highest density of rhinos. Experience another wildlife adventure and admire the views. Return to Guwahati and depart.

Package 6: Rajasthan

Rajasthan is a state in western India that offers a rich cultural and natural diversity. Here is a possible itinerary for a 3 days trip to Rajasthan:

Day 1: Arrive in Jaipur, the capital city and the pink city of India. Visit the Hawa Mahal, the Amer Fort, and the Jal Mahal, which are architectural marvels of the Rajput era. Enjoy the street food and shopping at the bazaars.

Day 2: Drive to Jodhpur, the blue city and the second largest city of Rajasthan. Visit the Mehrangarh Fort, the Umaid Bhawan Palace, and the Rao Jodha Desert Rock Park, which showcase the royal and natural heritage of the state.

Day 3: Drive to Jaisalmer, the golden city and the gateway to the Thar Desert. Visit the Jaisalmer Fort, the Patwon Ki Haveli, and the Gadisar Lake, which are examples of the medieval craftsmanship and culture of Rajasthan. Experience a camel safari and a desert camp in the sand dunes.

Package 7: West Bengal

West Bengal is a state in eastern India with a rich culture, stunning nature, and a vibrant past. Here is a possible itinerary for a 5 days trip:

Day 1: Arrive in Kolkata, the capital and financial hub of India. Explore the landmarks, such as the Howrah Bridge, the Victoria Memorial, and the Indian Museum. Enjoy the food and nightlife of Kolkata.

Day 2: Take a day trip to Gangasagar, an island in the Bay of Bengal that is sacred by Hindus. Visit the Kapil Muni Temple and take a dip in the confluence of the Ganga and the sea.

Day 3: Drive to Darjeeling, a hill station with views of the Himalayas. Visit the Lloyd’s Botanical Garden, which has rare plants and flowers. Also visit the Peace Pagoda, a Buddhist stupa that promotes peace.

Day 4: Take an early morning ride on the Darjeeling Himalayan Railway, a UNESCO site that offers a scenic journey through the mountains. Visit the Tiger Hill, where you can see a sunrise over the Kanchenjunga peak. Also visit the Ghum Monastery, one of the oldest Tibetan Buddhist monasteries in India.

Day 5: Drive to Siliguri, a city that connects West Bengal with other northeastern states. Visit the Salugara Monastery, which has a 100-feet high stupa with relics of Buddhist masters. Also visit the Madhuban Park, a tranquil garden by the Indian Army. Depart for your onward journey.
