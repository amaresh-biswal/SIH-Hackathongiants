Package 1: Maharastra

Maharashtra is a state in western India that offers a rich cultural and natural diversity. Here is a possible itinerary for a 5 days trip to Maharashtra:

Day 1: Arrive in Mumbai, the capital city and the financial hub of India. Explore the iconic landmarks, such as the Gateway of India, the Chhatrapati Shivaji Terminus, and the Mani Bhavan, where Mahatma Gandhi stayed. Enjoy the street food and nightlife of Mumbai.

Day 2: Take a day trip to Lonavala, a scenic hill station about 90 km from Mumbai. Visit the Karla Caves, a complex of ancient Buddhist rock-cut temples. Enjoy the panoramic views of the Western Ghats and the waterfalls.

Day 3: Drive to Nashik, a holy city on the banks of the Godavari river. Visit the Trimbakeshwar Temple, one of the twelve Jyotirlingas of Lord Shiva. Explore the vineyards and wineries of Nashik, and taste some of the finest wines in India.

Day 4: Drive to Aurangabad, a historical city that is the gateway to the Ajanta and Ellora caves. Visit the Ajanta Caves, a UNESCO World Heritage Site that showcases exquisite paintings and sculptures of Buddhist art. Visit the Ellora Caves, another UNESCO World Heritage Site that features rock-cut temples of Hinduism, Buddhism, and Jainism.

Day 5: Return to Mumbai and depart for your onward journey.

Package 4: Himachal Pradesh

Himachal Pradesh is a state in northern India that offers scenic beauty, cultural diversity, and adventure activities. Here is a possible itinerary for a 7 days trip to Himachal Pradesh:

Day 1: Arrive in Chandigarh and drive to Shimla, the capital of Himachal Pradesh and a popular hill station. Check in to your hotel and explore the Mall Road, the Ridge, and the Jakhu Temple.

Day 2: Take a day trip to Kufri, a nearby town that offers snow activities, wildlife park, and panoramic views. Return to Shimla and visit the Viceregal Lodge, the former residence of the British Viceroy.

Day 3: Drive to Manali, another famous hill station that is known for its natural beauty and adventure sports. En route, visit the Pandoh Dam, the Beas-Sutlej Link Project, and the Kullu Valley. Check in to your hotel and relax.

Day 4: Visit the Hadimba Devi Temple, a unique wooden temple dedicated to a mythological character. Also visit the Manu Temple, the Vashisht Temple, and the Tibetan Monastery. Enjoy shopping at the Mall Road and the Old Manali market.

Day 5: Take a day trip to Solang Valley, a picturesque spot that offers paragliding, skiing, zorbing, and ropeway rides. You can also visit the Rohtang Pass, a high mountain pass that connects Manali to Lahaul and Spiti valleys (subject to weather and permit conditions).

Day 6: Drive to Dharamshala, a serene town that is home to the Dalai Lama and the Tibetan government-in-exile. En route, visit the Baijnath Temple, a 13th-century temple dedicated to Lord Shiva. Check in to your hotel and enjoy the evening at leisure.

Day 7: Visit the McLeod Ganj, a suburb of Dharamshala that is also known as Little Lhasa. Visit the Namgyal Monastery, the Tsuglagkhang Complex, the Bhagsunag Temple, and the Dal Lake. Drive back to Chandigarh and depart for your onward journey.

Day 6: Drive to Alchi and visit the Alchi Monastery, known for its ancient art and architecture.

Day 7: Depart from Leh with unforgettable memories of Ladakh's breathtaking landscapes and rich culture.
